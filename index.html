Aesthetics of Exclusion:
Text-Mining Speer’s Rhetoric and Multimodal Analysis of Nazi Art Archives

Project Investigator: tykim05 (KAIST Undergrad)

Abstract: This installation integrates computational linguistics with an interactive curatorial gateway. By contrasting Albert Speer’s mathematical text metrics with raw visual propaganda archives and interactive user simulation, we demonstrate how the Third Reich weaponized abstract concepts of "beauty, purity, and absolute order" to camouflage the violent mechanics of socio-cultural liquidation.

II. Quantitative Text-Mining: Deconstructing Speer’s "Facts"

In his 1938 programmatic tract, "Facts and Lies" (tatsachen.htm), Reich Architect Albert Speer defended authoritarian scales under the semantic guise of objective structural "Truth." Computational string-frequency mapping illuminates how fascist architecture systematically swapped political violence with sterile words of cosmic order.

III. Visual Juxtaposition: Canonical vs. Degenerate Archives

The linguistic sanitized criteria observed in Speer’s text directly dictated the physical curation of visual fine arts. The state canonized sterile heroic realism while aggressively organizing public shaming exhibitions to liquidate modernism.

🏛️ CANONICAL ART RECORD (Official Reich Fine Art)
Rhetorical Sync Aligned with Speer's vocabulary of "Purity, Order, and Eternal Form"
Visual Motif Neo-classical statuary, idealized agrarian labor, and mathematically static landscapes devoid of raw individual emotion.
🔥 DEGENERATE ART RECORD (Banned Modernism)
Rhetorical Sync Branded as the chaotic antithesis—labeled as "Decay, Chaos, and Lies"
Visual Motif Expressionism, psychological distortion, and profound avant-garde critiques exposing the traumas of militarism.
